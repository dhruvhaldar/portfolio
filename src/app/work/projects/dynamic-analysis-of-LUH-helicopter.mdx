## Overview

This research investigates the dynamic behavior of the horizontal stabilizer and vertical fin of [HAL Light Utility Helicopter (LUH)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HAL_Light_Utility_Helicopter). The study employs finite element analysis (FEA) to determine the natural frequencies of these components and assess their stability in flight. Ensuring that these frequencies do not match rotor vibration frequencies is crucial for the safety and structural integrity of the helicopter.

## Key Features

- **Finite Element Analysis (FEA):** Conducted using HYPERMESH and NASTRAN to determine modal characteristics.
- **Material and Design Considerations:** Composite fiber materials used for weight reduction and improved performance.
- **Natural Frequency Calculation:** Comparison with rotor vibration frequencies to ensure stability.
- **Structural Stability Evaluation:** Identifying potential resonance risks and ensuring the components' safety in dynamic conditions.

## Technologies Used

- **HYPERMESH:** For finite element meshing and model creation.
- **NASTRAN:** For conducting modal analysis and obtaining natural frequencies.
- **MATLAB:** Used for computing natural frequency and time response curves.
- **Cheetah and Chetak Helicopter Systems:** Reference models used for transmission and structural behavior comparisons..

## Challenges and Learnings

- **Complexity of Helicopter Dynamics:** Understanding the interaction of structural components with rotor-induced vibrations.
- **Finite Element Modeling Accuracy:** Ensuring precise mesh generation and material property assignment for reliable results.
- **Computational Limitations:** Balancing computational efficiency with high-fidelity analysis.
- **Real-world Validation:** Comparing theoretical findings with real-world helicopter performance data.
